GeoPredict

Data is the primary driver of the digital economy and the world’s most valuable commodity. While it is well understood how to capture and store data from Internet-connected sources, processing it is less clear—lots of data goes to waste. One data set which is particularly difficult to process is geolocation data (a set of GPS coordinates of a user device as it moves through space). Enter GeoPredict, a machine learning model I developed during my time as a research associate in the lab as a part of my stint as a research associate in the lab of the Western Engineer dean in association with an industry partner. Geopredict mines value from massive sets of geolocation data collected on a continuous basis by applying A.I techniques from the field of natural language processing.
